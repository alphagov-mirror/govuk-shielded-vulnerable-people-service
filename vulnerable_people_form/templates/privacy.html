Last updated: 30 April 2020

This service is run by the Cabinet Office, working with:

  • the Ministry of Housing, Communities & Local Government
  • the Department for Environment Food & Rural Affairs
  • the Department for Work & Pensions
  • the National Health Service (NHS)

We’ll use the personal information you provide through this service to help make sure that people with a medical condition that makes them extremely vulnerable to coronavirus get the support they need.

We’ll use it to check your needs against data provided to us by the NHS, and to contact you with further advice or information.

We’ll also use it to get in touch with you so we can get your feedback on the service and support you have received.

This privacy notice covers:

  • the online service, where you can enter the information that we need to get support to you where you live
  • the automated telephone service, where you can provide the same information over the phone
  • online surveys and other tools we may use to get your feedback on the service

Lawful basis for processing your personal information

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we are processing this information on the basis that we’re carrying out a task in the public interest and to protect the vital interests of people more vulnerable to coronavirus.

This lawful basis applies to:

  • information shared with us by the NHS
  • provided to us through the online service or the automated telephone service

Some of the information we hold is sensitive health and care information. This is classed as ‘special category’ personal data within the meaning of Schedule 1 to the Data Protection Act 2018, meaning we must state a further lawful basis for processing it. We’re also relying on substantial public interest and governmental purpose to process this information.

If you provide any information through the surveys or other tools we may use to get your feedback, we’ll process this information on the basis that we’re carrying out a task in the public interest.

What we do with your information

When you submit information through the online service or the automated telephone service, we will compare your information to information shared with us by the NHS.

We ask for information about you and those who are supporting you through the online service and the automated telephone service. We may ask for more information as part of your subsequent interactions with the service. We may also ask for your feedback on the service.

We’ll share relevant parts of your information with other government departments, NHS bodies, local authorities and other public sector and private sector organisations - depending on what’s necessary to get people the support they need.

We may share your personal information with other public sector bodies in order to make sure that we and they have accurate data on who is vulnerable to coronavirus and to make sure we get help to the people who need it.

We may share your personal information (except for confidential health and care information) with trusted supermarkets and other suppliers, so they can prioritise your orders if you have indicated that you need help getting basic supplies. When we do so, we make sure we only provide the minimum information that is required to get help to you as quickly as possible.

We may use your data to produce anonymised information and share this information with other government departments to improve services, understand changing patterns of support needs or identify new support needs. The data we share for this purpose won’t identify you personally.

How long we keep your information

We will only keep your personal information for as long as we need it:

  • to make sure that you get the support you need if you are a person with a medical condition which makes you vulnerable
  • to make sure this service is meeting overall demand from vulnerable people
  • to help with the overall government response to coronavirus

Where your data is processed and stored

Your personal data may be transferred outside the UK while being processed by the Cabinet Office. If this happens, we’ll make sure you’re given the same level of technical and legal data protection as you are within the UK.

How we protect your information

We’re committed to doing all that we can to keep your information secure. We have set up systems and processes to prevent unauthorised access to or disclosure of the data we collect about you – for example, we protect your data using varying levels of encryption. All third parties that process personal data as part of this online service and the automated telephone service are required to keep that data secure. Our data processor is Amazon Web Services, who provide cloud hosting services. We also work with Methods Consulting who assist with the design of the service.

Your rights

In certain circumstances you have the right to ask for your information to be deleted by contacting the data controller. We may need to refuse your request if the information is needed as part of the emergency response to coronavirus.

You also have the right to request:

  • information about how your personal information is processed, and to request a copy of that personal data
  • that any inaccuracies in your personal information are rectified without delay
  • that any incomplete personal information is updated - you can include the missing information in your request
  • that the processing of your personal information is restricted in certain circumstances - for example, where accuracy is contested

You also have the right to object to the processing of your data.

If you want to make any of these requests, contact us using the details below.

NHS Login

Please note that if you access our service using your NHS login details, the identity verification services are managed by NHS Digital. NHS Digital is the controller for any personal information you provided to NHS Digital to get an NHS login account and verify your identity, and uses that personal information solely for that single purpose. For this personal information, our role is a “processor” only and we must act under the instructions provided by NHS Digital (as the “controller”) when verifying your identity. To see NHS Digital’s Privacy Notice and Terms and Conditions, please click here. This restriction does not apply to the personal information you provide to us separately.

Changes to this notice

We may change this privacy notice. When we make changes to this notice, the ‘last updated’ date at the top of this page will also change. Any changes to this privacy notice will apply to you and your information immediately. If these changes affect how your personal information is processed, the Cabinet Office will take reasonable steps to make sure you know.
